# Time-For-Money Engagement Terms

Prepaid four-week cycle (or one-off four-hour session), one reserved
working session per week, work against the agreed priority lane. Rate and
capacity per cycle are set by the Stripe checkout or invoice accepted at
signup.

## 1. Purpose

Client is buying Provider's time. Provider will weigh the usual quality, cost,
and speed tradeoffs and use reasonable judgment to advance client's priorities
within the prepaid cycle, without guaranteeing any particular scope, feature,
or outcome.

## 2. Commercials

Engagements take one of two forms: (a) a recurring prepaid four-week cycle
billed as an auto-renewing subscription (ongoing retainer), or (b) a one-off
four-hour session billed at the then-current algorithmically priced rate.
Both are paid upfront and neither is refundable. A
cycle is a fixed four-week window, not a calendar month, so every cycle
contains the same number of reserved sessions. Work starts after cleared
payment. To end a recurring arrangement, client must
cancel before the next subscription payment recurs; any cycle already paid
for runs to completion. Client should give at least one session's lead time
before cancellation for context transfer; without it, client may need to
purchase a one-off session at the then-current rate for handoff work.

## 3. Recurring Weekly Session

Provider reserves one working session per week during each cycle. Each session
may be used as a live working session or as a Provider work block applied
independently to the client's agreed priority items. Reschedules should be
requested with at least 24 hours' notice where reasonably practicable. If a
live session does not occur and is not rescheduled, Provider may use the
reserved block for independent work. Unused prepaid time expires at the end of
the cycle and prepaid fees are non-refundable unless the parties agree
otherwise in writing.

## 4. Included

Work happens in the client's own repositories. Client owns the repositories
and any code changes committed to them. This is a time-for-money engagement
with no fixed deliverable; session time may be directed to any lawful
technical work the parties agree on. Provider's suggested highest and best
uses of session time are:

1. **Focused independent build time** in which Provider advances client's
   priority lane by producing software.
2. **Collaborative live pair-programming sessions** that capture client's
   subject-matter expertise directly into the codebase.
3. **AI tooling pair-sessions** intended to improve client's own proficiency
   with AI tooling and workflows.

Allocation across these uses is at client's direction.

## 5. Not Included

Not included: uptime commitments, SLAs, or managed support; security
audits, compliance programs, or vendor questionnaires; tax, legal, or
regulated professional advice; guaranteed feature completion; dedicated
infrastructure or standalone architecture work; full repo or platform
transfer, exclusivity, or white-label rights.

## 6. Rights And Reuse

Client retains ownership of client data, repositories, and materials. Provider
retains all right, title, and interest in Provider's pre-existing code, tools,
templates, prompts, automations, workflows, libraries, and know-how.

## 7. Confidentiality And Data Boundary

Each party uses reasonable care to protect the other party's non-public
business and technical information and uses it only for this engagement.
Regulated or specially protected data (PHI, ePHI, etc.) is out of scope
unless separately agreed in writing. Provider may retain generalized know-how
and lessons learned without disclosing client confidential information.

## 8. Future Work

Additional cycles, production deployment, hosted modules, transition work, or
expanded rights require separate written approval, invoice acceptance, or
Stripe checkout acceptance. Either party may choose not to continue beyond
the end of the current paid cycle.

## 9. Warranty And Liability

Provider performs the work in a professional and workmanlike manner. The work
is exploratory and may involve incomplete systems, changing dependencies, and
third-party constraints. Aggregate liability is capped at fees actually paid
under the engagement, and neither party is liable for indirect, consequential,
special, or lost-profit damages, to the maximum extent permitted by law.

## 10. Acceptance

Electronic signatures, invoice payment, and Stripe checkout acceptance against
a proposal or checkout that references these terms are valid acceptance to the
maximum extent permitted by law.
